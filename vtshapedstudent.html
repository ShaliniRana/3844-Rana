I am a VT-shaped student because...

T-Shaped Student

  1. Vertical Component:

    I have a depth of understanding within my field of Creative Writing. Through my critical reading and writing skills of major works in American literature and the writing of my own creative pieces, I am able to understand the complexities of humanity through an empathetic and artistic lens.

  2. Horizontal Component:

    I have a transdisciplinary knowledge and technical literacy, based on the classes I have taken, that spans between the arts: Creative Writing, Sociology, Fine Arts, Italian, Public Speaking, Global Affairs, Greek, and American Literature AND technology/sciences/business: Data Analytics, Java Programming, Information Systems, Accounting, Economics, Biology, Marketing, and Statistics.

The V-Shaped Student

Since my freshman year, my academic experiences have gone beyond the classroom through an Alternative Spring Break trip I took to do service for a week in West Virginia, Community Writing: a class in which I taught elementary-aged kids from a low-income community in Christiansburg creative writing skills, my semester long study abroad to Switzerland through the Presidential Global Scholars program, a spring break study abroad to Cuba this past semester hosted by Pamplin, and two international research articles I got published through the university. In addition to the interdiscplinary courses I have taken during my semesters at Tech, I also DJed for WUVT, our college radio station, which allowed me to learn technologies used in the studio (soundboards, turntables, mics) and express myself as a music-lover. I'm constantly in search of accumulating experiences that transcend different areas of the brain, in order to learn and grow as much as I can. In other words: I just want to do everything!

My Plans and Goals

I am a "jack of all trades, master of none" kind of person. I like to wear many hats, and hope to continue doing this in the future. I love learning on my own terms and want to absorb everything I can in this beautiful lifetime.

Right now, my goal is to finish my undergrad experience and internship experience strong, and in a way where I am proud of the work I've produced and the memories I have made. I might take a break after school to come back to NoVA, live at home, and try out some odd jobs, such as, bartending or being a barista. I hope to learn to master one kind of artistic form during this time--either some kind of dance or musical instrument (I wouldn't mind starting a band, since I used to sing and play guitar). I am considering going back to Virginia Tech after my break in order to earn another undergrad degree in a technical program. Right now, I am looking at the Computational Modeling and Data Analytics program that is brand new at VT. In addition to fostering my arts skills, I really want to learn to master the disciplines of math, computer science, and statistics, on my own terms.
